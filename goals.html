Goals

Hey there! I'm Jacob and I've been a student of IT at the Nova Scotia Community College for the best part of a year now. I hope to be a fully-fledged programmer by the time I complete my diploma, and enter the workforce as one. I'm a dedicated student and quick learner, eager to take on programming challenges!

Short Term Goals
Network with Members of the Industry Gain Employment Experience as a Student Broaden My Skillset Put My Skills to Work as a Volunteer Obtain My Programming Diploma
One of the key reasons to attend any learning institution, college or university, is to meet with like-minded people and potential employers. I will utilize the events and programs put in place by NSCC, such as job fairs and workshops, as well as meet-and-greets that go on outside the college. I intend to get my name into the industry both to veterans of the IT field and students like myself, eager to break in. I wish to get my foot in the door as soon as possible, to understand the skills necessary and the expectations of holding an IT job in the real world. I am a driven, quick learner, and I recognize that the technologies taught and studied in school are just as important as the broader life lessons acquired as a member of the workforce. As I am still studying at NSCC, my most crucial goal is to broaden my skillset. I currently have experience programming in Python, writing HTML and CSS, as well as some introductory knowledge of BASIC and JavaScript. Through my progression of the diploma, I will become familiar with Java, C++, advanced programming concepts, among other skills that will be invaluable to future employment and success. I have always felt close to my community, and help others when I can. After gaining more experience and skills in my area of study, I wish to give back in whatever I can through use of my programming talent. Perhaps creating a website for a local non-profit, or a game that is accessible to the disabled, or something else that will improve the lives of fellow Haligonians. My ultimate goal in the short-term is to complete the IT Programming Diploma and graduate from NSCC. The employer connections provided by the college as well as the expert instructors who design courses that drive home real world skills will be excellent tools with which to enter the workforce. This will also be important milestone, and bring on greater freedom and responsiblity.
Long Term Goals
Obtain a Fulfilling Career Contribute Meaningful Work Personal Growth Through My Career Achieve Financial Stability Travel the World
Looking further into the future, I hope to settle into a job that not only pays well or has a quick commute, but provides fulfillment and pride. Programming can be more than just work, whether that be creating art or a valuable tool, and I hope to be a part of such a project in my future. I want to create programs that can help others as well. Creating algorithms that can assist handicapped people, or games that can improve the lives of those with learning disabilities is an endeavour that I am eager to pursue. Using my skills to make things that can improve the lives of those less fortunate is something I am very dedicated to. I also want my career to challenge my perceptions, open me to new points of view, and introduce me to people from far and wide. People spend upwards of 40 years in their field between entering it and retiring, and I would hate to be the same person in the beginning and the end. As a student currently, finance is more a question of how much debt are you in as opposed to your net worth. I want a career that rewards me fairly for my contributions, and that I can support a family on. I aim for a job that allows me to own a home, a car and some luxuries besides. Lastly, I hope that upon building my name in the corporate world, I am given the opportunity to travel outside of Canada to see all different parts of the world. Whether through business or pleasure, traveling is one of my favourite activities and I believe an enormous amount can be learned from the culture and people of other nations. A whole lifetime spent in one town is a sheltered life indeed.
